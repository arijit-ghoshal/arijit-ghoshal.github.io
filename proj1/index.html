Project 1: Images of the Russian Empire

Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection.

Single-Scale Alignment for Low-Resolution Images

Here are the three JPG files which used single-scale alignment. I aligned both the red and green channels to the blue channel. For the red/green channels, I performed an exhaustive search over a reasonable range of shifts. For each shift, I aggressively cropped both the red/green and blue channels, retaining only the middle 80% in both dimensions, and calculated the Normalized Cross Correlation metric. The best shift was the one which maximized the correlation.

Multi-Scale Pyramid Alignment for High-Resolution Images

Here are the reamining eleven TIF images. I created an image pyramid scheme that began with a low resolution (256x256 pixels) and then iteratively updated the best shift by repeatedly upscaling the image by a factor of 2. I chose a reasonable initial shift range (25 pixels in each direction), and successive calls to the exhaustive align function required only 5 shifts in each direction. Notably, the emir image does not perform as well using the NCC metric.
